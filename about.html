About Nnamonu

Professor Lami Nnamonu is a distinguished Nigerian chemist with a focus on agrochemicals. She earned her PhD in Agrochemical Technology, the first in sub-Saharan Africa, and has since become a professor at the Federal University of Agriculture Makurdi (now Joseph Sarwuan Tarka University Makurdi). Her research helps improve agricultural productivity in the face of climate challenges.


Professor Nnamonu is also a skilled project manager. She currently leads the World Bank-funded Centre for Innovation in Procurement Environmental and Social Standards (CIPESS). Her strong work in this area was recognized with two awards at a project control summit in 2024.


Professor Nnamonu's dedication is further evidenced by her academic achievements. Her PhD thesis won a national award and she received postdoctoral fellowships from the Schlumberger Foundation. She is a respected scholar and the first female Professor of Chemistry in Benue State.


She’s married to Dr Emmanuel Nnamonu (MBBS) and they are blessed with five children.
